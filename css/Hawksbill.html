Hawksbill

Hawksbill turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata) is listed as a Critically Endangered by IUCN, their most comun threat is associated by the trade of the beautiful scutes of the carapace and plastron of this specie, this practice is named tortoiseshell and has been traded by human since pre-Christian times until now, another threat is egg collection, slaughter for meat, destruction of nesting habitat, destruction of foraging habitat, hybridisation of Hawksbill with other species, entanglement and ingestion of marine debris, and finally oil pollution.
